<section class="section">

    <ul class="feature-list">

      <li>Static workflows, not real-world exceptions</li>
      <li>Integration as plumbing, not as continuity</li>

<section class="section">
  <div class="wrapper">
    <h2 class="section-title">What Tefter Optimizes For</h2>
    <p class="section-copy">
      Tefter optimizes for recovery, control, and reality — starting with TX. Apps sit on top and augment ERP execution, but continuity comes first.
    </p>
    <div class="card-grid">
      <div class="card">
        <h3>Business-operable recovery</h3>
        <p>When data is wrong, the business can resolve it without waiting on IT.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <h3>Unified exchange + execution</h3>
        <p>One platform with a clear boundary: TX for continuity, apps for work.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <h3>Failure-first design</h3>
        <p>Assumes outages and mismatches — and keeps transactions safe through them.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="wrapper">
    <h2 class="section-title">Concrete Scenario</h2>
    <div class="card" style="margin: 30px 0; background: #f8f9fa;">
      <p><strong>Order:</strong> A sales rep submits an order from a customer visit.</p>
      <p><strong>Failure:</strong> The customer expects a different SKU and their endpoint is temporarily unavailable.</p>
      <p><strong>What changes with Tefter:</strong> TX holds the transaction safely, resumes automatically, and routes the mapping decision to the right business role.</p>
      <p><strong>Outcome:</strong> The order completes without manual re-entry and without an IT queue.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
