    <h1 class="section-title">Operational infrastructure — not an app</h1>
    <p class="section-copy">
      Tefter is built to keep real-world operations moving when exchange fails. It combines an Exchange Layer (TX) for continuity with an Operational Layer that helps teams execute daily work — alongside your ERP, not competing with it.
    </p>
    <div class="highlight-box">
      <p>One platform. Two layers. Any scale.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="wrapper">
    <h2 class="section-title">Why Traditional Architectures Fail</h2>
    <p class="section-copy">
      Most “integration” breaks in predictable ways — because it assumes a stable world.
    </p>
    <ul class="feature-list">
      <li>Point-to-point connections multiply — and any change becomes a project</li>
      <li>ERP workflows assume perfect inputs and stable partner systems</li>
      <li>Recovery is manual, slow, and invisible until the business feels it</li>
      <li>Business teams can’t resolve issues without IT in the middle</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="wrapper">
    <h2 class="section-title">TX as the System of Continuity</h2>
    <p class="section-copy">
      Tefter TX is the core: a recovery-first exchange layer for business transactions. It treats failure as normal, preserves execution state, and resumes work when systems recover — with business-operable resolution when data is wrong.
    </p>
    <div class="highlight-box">
      <p><strong>TX is not “integration.”</strong> It’s a continuity engine for real-world operations.</p>
    </div>
