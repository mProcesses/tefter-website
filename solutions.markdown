<section class="section">
  <div class="wrapper">
    <h2 class="section-title">Common Scenarios (Across Industries)</h2>
    <p class="section-copy">
      These failure modes show up everywhere. TX turns them into recoverable, business-operable workflows.
    </p>

    <div class="table-scroll-container">
      <table>
        <thead>
          <tr>
            <th>Pain point</th>
            <th>Scenario</th>
            <th>What TX does</th>
            <th>Business impact</th>
          </tr>
        </thead>
        <tbody>
          <tr>
            <td>Network failure</td>
            <td>Partner system is down mid-transaction</td>
            <td>TX pauses safely, retries automatically, and resumes from the right point</td>
            <td>Orders keep flowing — no manual re-entry, no “where did it go?” escalations</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Wrong mapping</td>
            <td>SKU / unit-of-measure mismatch</td>
            <td>TX flags the mismatch, suggests a correction, routes approval to the business, and replays the transaction</td>
            <td>Minutes to resolution — without an IT ticket</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Delayed invoices</td>
            <td>Invoice delivery fails or partner endpoint is intermittent</td>
            <td>TX retries automatically and keeps the transaction state visible and owned</td>
            <td>Faster close and fewer disputes</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Partner onboarding</td>
            <td>New supplier must transact today</td>
            <td>Register, get credentials, connect via EDI/API/files/email, and refine mappings as needed</td>
            <td>Live in hours (often minutes to first transaction)</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Multi-partner workflows</td>
            <td>Split fulfillment across warehouses and partners</td>
            <td>TX isolates partner failures, keeps each transaction recoverable, and provides end-to-end visibility</td>
            <td>Predictable operations, fewer errors, fewer fire-drills</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Shipping changes</td>
            <td>Routes change mid-day; partners need updated confirmations</td>
            <td>TX delivers updates reliably and resumes cleanly after partial failures</td>
            <td>Fewer missed deliveries and fewer “where is it?” calls</td>
          </tr>
        </tbody>
      </table>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
