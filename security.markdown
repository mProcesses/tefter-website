    <p class="section-copy">
      Tefter is built from the ground up with security, compliance, and governance as core principles. Every transaction, user action, and partner interaction is fully traceable, auditable, and controlled.
    </p>

</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="wrapper">
    <h2 class="section-title">Why Security Fails in Integration</h2>
    <p class="section-copy">
      In many organizations, “integration” spreads responsibility across too many systems — and security becomes unclear.
    </p>
    <ul class="feature-list">
      <li>Too many components and vendors, unclear ownership</li>
      <li>Fragmented audit trails across systems</li>
      <li>Partner access that grows over time without governance</li>
      <li>Incidents that require manual correlation and recovery</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="wrapper">
    <h2 class="section-title">Governance Model</h2>
    <p class="section-copy">
      Tefter provides clear boundaries of responsibility: roles, partner access, and auditability — so operational control doesn’t become security risk.
    </p>
    <div class="card-grid">
      <div class="card">
        <h3>Roles & boundaries</h3>
        <p>Role-based access separates responsibilities across Sales, Procurement, Warehouse, Partner Management, and Admin.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <h3>Auditability</h3>
        <p>Every meaningful action is logged with who/what/when — so reviews are defensible.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <h3>Partner governance</h3>
        <p>Partner access is explicit, scoped, and reviewable — not hidden in ad-hoc integrations.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="wrapper">
    <h2 class="section-title">Incident & Compliance Scenarios</h2>
    <div class="card-grid">
      <div class="card">
        <h3>Credential compromise</h3>
        <p><strong>What happens:</strong> A partner credential is suspected to be leaked.</p>
        <p><strong>Response:</strong> Access can be revoked, activity audited, and impact scoped with clear logs.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <h3>Audit / compliance review</h3>
        <p><strong>What happens:</strong> Security asks: “Who approved this change and when?”</p>
        <p><strong>Response:</strong> Audit trails provide traceability for approvals, configuration changes, and partner interactions.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <h3>Operational incident</h3>
        <p><strong>What happens:</strong> A transaction fails mid-flow with partial success.</p>
        <p><strong>Response:</strong> The incident is visible, contained, and recoverable — with a clear next action and owner.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
